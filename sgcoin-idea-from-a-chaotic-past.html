SGCoin - idea from a chaotic past

lwx 13 Nov 2021 on Sgcoin, Idea, Past

SGCoin

For on and off in the past 4-5 years, I have been thinking and tinkering with an idea.

It is crypto related, and I call it SGCoin.

The idea is simple, but certainly not easy to execute.

We have a crypto coin, called SGCoin (SGDC), it is stable in nature.

1 SGDC == 1 SGD

To stablize it, we offer an exchange service, you can always exchange SG$1 for 1 SGDC and vice versa.

Anyone who offers SGDC at a higher or lower rate will be arbitrage into a loss.

We reach out to merchants and offer 0% fees in payment services. And of course, fast, 0% transfer with no boundaries.

As we build an eco-system around this digital currency, and create utility. Users will in time, prefer having this asset in their wallets than SG Dollars, since it will be more useful and convenient.

A few things have changed since my inception of this idea back in 2017-2018.

Differences between now and then

Initially, SGCoin was meant to be a centralized coin. That way, we didn’t have to reward miners/validators for transactions. And we could get away by hosting our own nodes.

However, these days, I gravitate towards an ERC-20 token, with a bridge to non-crypto (uninterested in crypto) users.

The business model back then was to emulate banks and take the float and invest them in risk-free securities.

Currently, I feel that an easier and safer way is to simply start charging merchants. But provide a service/ecosystem that is so good that it justifies the charge.

Presentation back in 2017-2018

I gave a presentation to some folks back in 2017-2018 to get some feedback, can’t remember the exact date, slides can be found at the end of the post.

In hindsight, I was definitely talking to the wrong audience. I didn’t realize this then but some people are contented to be in a 9-5 dead end job.

For them listening to my presentation, it was probably more of an amusement.

A few of the comments stuck with me:

  1. People don’t like digital cash, they like “real” cash.

    Well, pretty clear it’s the other way round.

  2. What happens to old people who cannot use a phone?

    We are not trying to boil the ocean. Do you ask Google what happens to people who cannot navigate the internet in this time and age?

  3. The logo is ugly, (snickers), you should not try to do this again.

The guy who said this is a joke. That is what is known as a placeholder, but obviously that is beyond the comprehension of his single digit IQ.

My takeaways

Do not willy nilly talk to others about your ideas, and definitely do not take them seriously. Most people do not want to see you try - for fear that you may actually succeed.

Of course, hindsight is 20/20. I wish I had continued the course, maybe it would have become a reality.

Reflections of the idea (from my perspective)

Back then, lightning network was relatively new. And there was no application of it yet, now in El Salvador, we can see it working perfectly, which proves its feasibility.

Lightning network or the idea of it, is something worth exploring. I keep thinking of offline payments, like when we use fiat notes, we don’t require network connectivity. We could do the same with crypto multi-sig channels, there is definitely some value there.

Short DBS crypto story

Back in 2017, I registered for DBS’s Management Associate program and got invited to their seminar(?) - where you get to talk to current MAs and they evaluate you basically. Most of the other folks I spoke to was there for the free food, just like myself.

When one of the MAs approached my group, I decided to ask her about her thoughts on Bitcoin, and why would people still use wire transfers. She gave a bewildered look and said Bitcoin just won’t work (with no explanation of why), and promptly left to mingle with another group.

Clearly, she had no idea what Bitcoin or crypto was.

And this isn’t new, when I was working at a brokerage, I used to have so many heated discussions with my then AVP, whom is a nice guy but really despises and doubts crypto. I would try to convince him and dispel his doubts. But to no avail.

Sometimes, you may think that people from finance/banking will be most equipped to handle crypto-related conversations. But the truth is, they are cemented in their archaic way of thinking, which has gotten themselves to a good position in life. And when something disruptive or innovative appears, they are perhaps too quick to dismiss it.

Disclaimer

I didn’t and still don’t have any crypto holdings. I am purely fascinated by the applications of crypto and the DeFi scene.
